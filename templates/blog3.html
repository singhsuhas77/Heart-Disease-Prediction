Exercise & Heart Health

Regular physical activity is crucial for maintaining a healthy heart. Exercise helps lower blood pressure, control weight, and improve overall cardiovascular health.

Benefits of Exercise for Heart Health

Engaging in regular physical activity provides several benefits for heart health:

  • • Lowers blood pressure and improves circulation.
  • • Reduces bad cholesterol (LDL) and increases good cholesterol (HDL).
  • • Helps control weight and reduces the risk of obesity.
  • • Enhances heart muscle strength and endurance.
  • • Reduces stress and improves mental health.

Best Exercises for a Healthy Heart

Incorporate these exercises into your routine for optimal heart health:

  • • Aerobic Exercises: Walking, jogging, cycling, and swimming.
  • • Strength Training: Lifting weights and resistance exercises.
  • • Flexibility & Balance: Yoga and stretching exercises.
  • • High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT): Short bursts of intense exercise.

Exercise Guidelines

Follow these guidelines for a safe and effective workout:

  • • Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week.
  • • Include both aerobic and strength training exercises.
  • • Warm up before and cool down after each session.
  • • Listen to your body and avoid overexertion.
  • • Stay hydrated and maintain a balanced diet.
