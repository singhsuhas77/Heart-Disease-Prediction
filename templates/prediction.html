Heart Disease Prediction

Attribute Descriptions:

Age
Age in years
Gender
Biological sex (Male/Female)
Chest Pain Type
  • Typical Angina: Chest pain related to decreased blood supply to the heart
  • Atypical Angina: Chest pain not meeting criteria for angina
  • Non-anginal Pain: Chest pain not caused by heart disease
  • Asymptomatic: No chest pain symptoms
Resting Blood Pressure
Blood pressure measurement in mmHg at rest (normal range: 90-120 mmHg)
Serum Cholesterol
Cholesterol measurement in mg/dl (desirable: <200 mg/dl)
Fasting Blood Sugar
Blood sugar after fasting (>120 mg/dl indicates possible diabetes)
Resting ECG Results
  • Normal: No abnormalities
  • ST-T wave abnormality: May indicate ischemia or other conditions
  • Left ventricular hypertrophy: Thickening of heart's left ventricle
Max Heart Rate
Maximum heart rate achieved during exercise (typically 220 minus age)
Exercise-induced Angina
Chest pain during physical activity (indicates possible coronary artery disease)
ST Depression
ST segment depression induced by exercise relative to rest (measured in mm)
Slope of ST Segment
  • Upsloping: Generally normal
  • Flat: May indicate ischemia
  • Downsloping: May indicate significant coronary artery disease
Number of Major Vessels
Number of major blood vessels (0-4) colored by fluoroscopy (indicates blockages)
Thalassemia
  • Normal: No blood disorder
  • Fixed Defect: Previous heart damage
  • Reversible Defect: Blood flow issues during exercise
